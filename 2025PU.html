Asteroid 2025 PU

Asteroid 2025 PU is a newly observed near-Earth object that belongs to the Apollo group of asteroids, known for having orbits that cross Earth’s path. It appears to be a small to mid-sized rocky body that travels on an elongated orbit around the Sun, occasionally bringing it close to our planet. Its discovery highlights the constant monitoring of the sky for objects that move in near-Earth space, and while it follows a predictable path, its future flybys are studied carefully to refine its orbit and understand any potential risks. Like other near-Earth asteroids, it offers insights into the composition and movement of primitive solar system material..

Key Facts

  • Size: Estimated between 2.1 and 4.8 meters in diameter.
  • Closest Approach: Passed extremely close to Earth at 53,000km/h.
  • Speed: Traveling at a high speed typical for inner solar system objects.
  • Orbit: Semi-major axis of 1.49AU,with a perihelion of 0.896AU and an aphelion of 2.088AU.
