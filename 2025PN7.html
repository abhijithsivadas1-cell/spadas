Asteroid 2025 PN7 - Earth's Quasi-Moon

Asteroid 2025 PN7 is a near-Earth object and Earth’s newest quasi-moon, discovered on August 2, 2025, by the Pan-STARRS 1 telescope at Haleakalā Observatory, Hawaii. Measuring approximately 19 meters in diameter, this small, rocky asteroid follows a solar orbit closely synchronized with Earth, creating the appearance of orbiting our planet without being a true moon. Its orbit is slightly inclined (~2°) and mildly eccentric (0.108), with a semi-major axis of about 1.003 AU, completing a revolution around the Sun roughly every year. 2025 PN7 has likely been in this quasi-satellite configuration for around 60 years and is expected to remain in it for another century, after which it will transition to a different orbit. Although faint and difficult to observe due to its small size and apparent magnitude (~26.4), it provides a unique opportunity for studying the dynamics of near-Earth asteroids, orbital resonances, and temporary co-orbital objects, contributing to our understanding of potential mini-moons and the gravitational interactions between small bodies and Earth..

Key Facts

  • Type: Near-Earth Asteroid, Quasi-satellite of Earth.
  • Diameter: 19 meters (62 feet).
  • Orbit: Semi-major axis 1.003 AU, meaning it is in a near-orbital resonance with Earth.
  • Closest Approach: 299,000 km from Earth.
