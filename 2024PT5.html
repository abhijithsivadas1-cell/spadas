Asteroid 2024 PT5

Asteroid 2024 PT5 is a near-Earth object (NEO) that belongs to the Apollo group, meaning its orbit crosses that of Earth. It is relatively small, estimated to be tens of meters in diameter, making it comparable to a large building or small hill. Its surface is likely rocky, typical of stony S-type asteroids, and it reflects a modest amount of sunlight, giving it a relatively low albedo. 2024 PT5 follows an eccentric orbit around the Sun, occasionally bringing it close to Earth, though current observations indicate it poses no significant impact threat in the near future. Its trajectory and speed are monitored carefully to refine predictions, as even small asteroids like this can provide valuable scientific insight into the composition and dynamics of early solar system material..

Key Facts

  • Size: Approximately 11 meters (36 feet) in diameter (roughly the size of a school bus).
  • Orbit: Followed a horseshoe-shaped path around Earth for nearly two months.
  • Distance: Closest approach was about 1.5 million kilometers, roughly four times the average distance between Earth and the Moon.
