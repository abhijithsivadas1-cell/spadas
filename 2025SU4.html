Asteroid 2025 SU4

Asteroid 2025 SU4 is a newly observed near-Earth object belonging to the **Apollo group** of asteroids, meaning its orbit around the Sun crosses Earth’s path, making it an object of interest for planetary defense monitoring. It is a relatively small compared to larger asteroids but still significant enough to be tracked closely due to its Earth-approaching trajectory.With its elongated orbit, 2025 SU4 travels through the inner solar system, moving between the regions near earth and beyond, influenced by gravitational interactions with our planet and nearby celestial bodies. Its surface composition is likely rocky, resembling other stony asteroids, and it reflects sunlight faintly, which is how astronomers were able to detect and analyseit. While not considered a large scale threat, its orbital pattern places it in the category of objects that require continuous observation to better predict any future close approaches or potential risks.

Key Facts

  • Size: Estimated to be between 2 and 5 meters (6.6–16.4 feet) in diameter.
  • Speed: Traveling at approximately $9.18\text{ km/s}$ ($33,000\text{ km/h}$ or $20,500\text{ mph}$) relative to Earth.
  • Orbit: Belongs to the Aten group of asteroids, which have orbits that bring them close to Earth's path.
