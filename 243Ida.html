243 Ida - Main-Belt Asteroid

243 Ida is a fascinating main-belt asteroid, discovered on September 29, 1884, by Johann Palisa. It measures roughly 15.7 × 9.2 × 8.5 kilometers and has an irregular, elongated shape, indicative of a history of collisions and fragmentation. Ida is a stony S-type asteroid, composed primarily of silicate rock and nickel-iron, with a moderately reflective surface that gives it an albedo of about 0.22. It orbits the Sun every 4.84 years at a distance ranging from 2.2 to 2.9 AU, with an orbital eccentricity of 0.28 and an inclination of about 9.9°. Ida gained prominence when the Galileo spacecraft flew by it in 1993, revealing its heavily cratered surface and discovering its small moon, Dactyl, making it the first asteroid known to have a natural satellite. Its surface features suggest a long history of impacts and regolith development, providing valuable insights into the early solar system and the processes that shaped asteroids in the main belt.

Key Facts

  • Diameter: Approximately 15.7 × 9.2 × 8.5 km (irregular shape).
  • Orbital Category: Main-belt asteroid (between Mars and Jupiter).
  • Orbital Period: About 4.84 years.
  • Moon: Has a small moon named Dactyl, discovered in 1993 by the Galileo spacecraft.
  • Discovery: Johann Palisa on September 29, 1884.
