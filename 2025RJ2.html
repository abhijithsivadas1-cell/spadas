Asteroid 2024 RJ2

Asteroid 2024 RJ2 is a near earth object identified as part of the Apollo-Class asteroids, meaning its orbit intersects Earth's orbit as it circles the sun. It is relatively small in size but scientifically important because of its close approaches that bring it within Earth’s neighborhood. Its path around the Sun is elongated, and its trajectory is influenced by the gravitational pulls of planets. Discovered by the Pan-STARRS 1 telescope, it is composed mainly of rocky material.

Key Facts

  • Size: Approximately 12 feet (4 meters) in diameter.
  • Discovery: First observed on September 2, 2025, by the Pan-STARRS 1 telescope.
  • Close Approaches: Passed within about $187,000\text{ miles}$ ($301,000\text{ km}$) of Earth and even closer to the Moon ($8,000\text{ miles} / 13,000\text{ km}$).
  • Orbit: Belongs to the Apollo group of asteroids, which have orbits that cross Earth's path.
