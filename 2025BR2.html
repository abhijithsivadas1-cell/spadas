Asteroid 2025 BR2

Asteroid 2025 BR2 is a near-Earth object that has drawn attention due to its placement within the Apollo group of asteroids, whose orbits extend across Earth’s path around the Sun. It follows a stretched, elliptical orbit that occasionally brings it into Earth’s vicinity, making it part of the set of objects astronomers track for planetary safety. Like many recently discovered asteroids, 2025 BR2 is not just studied for its potential approach but also as a way to refine our understanding of the population of small bodies moving through near-Earth space. Observing and cataloging such asteroids helps scientists improve models of orbital evolution, impact probabilities, and the long-term behavior of these rocky remnants of the solar system’s formation..

Key Facts

  • Size: Approximately 67 feet (20.4 meters) in length.
  • Speed: Traveling at about 24,000 mph (38,600 km/h).
  • Close Approach: Passed at a distance of approximately 3.21 million miles from Earth (about 13 times the distance to the Moon).
