Asteroid 2025 FA22

Asteroid 2025 FA22 is a near-Earth object discovered by the Pan-STARRS-2 survey in 2025 and is estimated to be around 160 meters in diameter, with possible size variations between about 125 and 275 meters. Its orbit places it in the category of a Potentially Hazardous Asteroid (PHA) because it is both relatively large and follows a path that can bring it close to Earth. The asteroid’s orbit is elongated and slightly tilted compared to Earth’s, with moderate eccentricity and inclination, making it an important target for continued study. Its size and classification make it significant for planetary defense research, as understanding its physical properties and orbital behavior helps refine impact predictions and enhances preparedness strategies for near-Earth objects..

Key Details

  • Size: Estimated between 130 and 290 meters in diameter (larger than Delhi's Qutub Minar).
  • Speed: Traveling at approximately 24,000 mph (38,600 km/h).
  • Closest Approach: Came within 520,000 miles (835,000 km) of Earth, which is just over twice the distance to the Moon.
