Asteroid 2024 ON

Asteroid 2024 ON is a near-Earth object approximately 350 meters (1,150 feet) in diameter, roughly the size of two football fields. It was discovered on July 27, 2024, by the ATLAS-MLO telescope on Mauna Kea, Hawaii. On September 17, 2024, it made a close approach to Earth, passing at a distance of about 620,000 miles (1 million kilometers)—approximately 2.6 times the average distance between Earth and the Moon. Despite its size and proximity, 2024 ON posed no threat to our planet. Radar observations revealed that it is a contact binary, meaning it consists of two lobes that are physically connected. This close encounter underscored the importance of monitoring large near-Earth objects to assess potential risks and develop mitigation strategies..
